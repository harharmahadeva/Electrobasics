LESSON PURPOSE
============================================================

This lesson introduces capacitors as electronic components that store and release electrical energy.
Beginners will learn what capacitors look like, what they do, where they are used, how polarity matters, and how to handle them safely.

============================================================
BEGINNER-FRIENDLY THEORY | शुरुआती सिद्धांत
============================================================

English

A capacitor is a passive electronic component that stores electrical energy in an electric field.

Simple idea:
A capacitor is like a tiny rechargeable storage tank for electric charge.

It does not store energy like a big battery for a long time.
It stores and releases charge quickly.

------------------------------------------------------------
BASIC STRUCTURE
------------------------------------------------------------

A capacitor usually has:
• Two conductive plates
• An insulating material between them called dielectric
• Two leads or terminals

When voltage is applied:
• One plate collects positive charge.
• The other plate collects negative charge.
• Energy is stored in the electric field between the plates.

------------------------------------------------------------
UNIT OF CAPACITANCE
------------------------------------------------------------

Capacitance is the ability to store charge.

Unit:
Farad (F)

But 1 Farad is very large for most beginner circuits.
Common smaller units are:

microfarad = µF
nanofarad = nF
picofarad = pF

Common conversion:
1 µF = 1000 nF
1 nF = 1000 pF
1 µF = 1,000,000 pF

Examples:
100 µF is common in power supply filtering.
10 µF is common in small circuits.
100 nF is very common for decoupling near ICs.
22 pF is common around crystal oscillators.

------------------------------------------------------------
WHAT DOES A CAPACITOR DO?
------------------------------------------------------------

A capacitor can:
• Store charge
• Smooth power supply voltage
• Reduce noise
• Block DC but pass changing signals
• Create time delays with resistors
• Help motors start
• Stabilize circuits

------------------------------------------------------------
CAPACITOR CHARGE AND DISCHARGE
------------------------------------------------------------

Charging:
When connected to a voltage source, the capacitor fills with charge.

Discharging:
When connected to a load, it releases stored charge.

Water analogy:
A capacitor is like a small water tank.
It fills when water pressure is available.
It can release water briefly when supply drops.

Electronics analogy:
A capacitor fills with charge when voltage is present.
It releases charge when the circuit needs it.

------------------------------------------------------------
POLARIZED VS NON-POLARIZED CAPACITORS
------------------------------------------------------------

Non-polarized capacitors:
• Can be connected either way.
• Examples: ceramic, film capacitors.
• Usually small values.
• Often used for noise filtering and signals.

Polarized capacitors:
• Must be connected in the correct direction.
• Examples: electrolytic and tantalum capacitors.
• Have positive and negative terminals.
• Used for higher capacitance values.
• Wrong polarity can damage or even burst the capacitor.

Important:
Electrolytic capacitors usually show a stripe marking the negative side.
The longer lead may indicate positive on some through-hole capacitors.

------------------------------------------------------------
VOLTAGE RATING
------------------------------------------------------------

A capacitor has a voltage rating.
This is the maximum voltage it can safely handle.

Example:
A capacitor marked 100µF 25V means:
• Capacitance = 100 microfarads
• Maximum safe voltage = 25 volts

Beginner rule:
Always use a capacitor voltage rating higher than the circuit voltage.

Example:
For a 5V circuit, a 10V, 16V or 25V capacitor can be safe.
Do not use a 6.3V capacitor close to its limit unless you understand the design.

------------------------------------------------------------
COMMON CAPACITOR TYPES
------------------------------------------------------------

1. Ceramic Capacitor
Small, non-polarized, cheap, good for noise filtering.
Common values: 10nF, 100nF.

2. Electrolytic Capacitor
Polarized, larger capacitance, used in power supply smoothing.
Common values: 1µF, 10µF, 100µF, 1000µF.

3. Film Capacitor
Non-polarized, stable, used in signal and timing circuits.

4. Tantalum Capacitor
Polarized, compact, stable, but sensitive to wrong polarity.

5. Supercapacitor
Very high capacitance, stores much more energy than normal capacitors.

------------------------------------------------------------
CAPACITOR SYMBOLS
------------------------------------------------------------

Non-polarized capacitor symbol:
Two parallel plates.

Polarized capacitor symbol:
One straight plate and one curved plate, or plus sign shown near positive terminal.

------------------------------------------------------------
CAPACITOR MARKINGS
------------------------------------------------------------

Large electrolytic capacitors often print values directly:
100µF 25V

Small ceramic capacitors often use codes:
104 = 100,000 pF = 100nF = 0.1µF
103 = 10,000 pF = 10nF
102 = 1,000 pF = 1nF
221 = 220 pF

Beginner note:
Capacitor code reading will be practiced lightly here.
A later measurement lesson will cover testing components more deeply.

============================================================
STEP-BY-STEP: IDENTIFYING A CAPACITOR
============================================================

Step 1:
Look at the shape.

Small disc = often ceramic capacitor.
Cylindrical can = often electrolytic capacitor.
Box-shaped = often film capacitor.

Step 2:
Check if it has polarity.

Electrolytic capacitor:
• Usually has a stripe marking negative side.
• Often has longer positive lead.

Ceramic capacitor:
• Usually no polarity.
• Can be connected either way.

Step 3:
Read the value.

Direct marking:
100µF 25V

Code marking:
104 = 100nF

Step 4:
Check voltage rating.

Never use a capacitor below the circuit voltage.

Step 5:
Place safely in the circuit.

For polarized capacitor:
Positive to higher voltage side.
Negative to ground or lower voltage side.

Lab Name:
Observe Capacitor Charging and LED Fade

Required Components:
• Breadboard
• LED
• 330Ω resistor
• 100µF electrolytic capacitor
• 5V power source or 3V battery pack
• Jumper wires
• Digital multimeter

Safety:
• Use low voltage only.
• Check capacitor polarity.
• Do not use capacitor voltage rating below supply voltage.
• Do not short large capacitors.
• Discharge capacitor safely through a resistor.

Circuit Idea:
Battery + → resistor → LED → ground
Capacitor connected across power rails:
Capacitor positive to + rail
Capacitor negative to ground rail

Steps:
1. Place the LED and resistor on the breadboard.
2. Connect power through the resistor to LED.
3. Confirm LED lights normally.
4. Turn power OFF.
5. Add electrolytic capacitor across power rails with correct polarity.
6. Turn power ON.
7. Turn power OFF and observe if LED fades briefly.
8. Try a larger capacitor if available.
9. Measure capacitor voltage using DMM.
10. Record observations.

============================================================
COMMON BEGINNER MISTAKES | सामान्य शुरुआती गलतियाँ
============================================================

• Reversing electrolytic capacitor polarity.
• Ignoring voltage rating.
• Thinking capacitor is the same as a battery.
• Shorting capacitor leads directly.
• Using a polarized capacitor in an AC signal path without understanding.
• Confusing µF, nF and pF.
• Reading 104 as 104µF instead of 100nF.
• Measuring capacitance while capacitor is connected in a circuit.
• Touching charged large capacitors without discharging them.
• Using a damaged or bulging capacitor.

============================================================
TROUBLESHOOTING GUIDE | समस्या समाधान
============================================================

Problem:
Capacitor gets hot.

Possible Causes:
• Wrong polarity.
• Voltage rating too low.
• Faulty capacitor.
• Circuit wiring error.

Action:
Turn power OFF immediately.

Problem:
LED does not fade.

Possible Causes:
• Capacitor value too small.
• Capacitor not connected correctly.
• LED circuit path is wrong.
• Capacitor is damaged.

Problem:
Multimeter reading is unstable.

Possible Causes:
• Capacitor charging/discharging during measurement.
• Poor probe contact.
• Wrong meter mode.

Problem:
Capacitor value seems different from marking.

Explanation:
Capacitors often have wider tolerance than resistors.
Some electrolytic capacitors may vary significantly and still be normal.

Problem:
Electrolytic capacitor has a bulging top.

Action:
Do not use it. Replace it.

============================================================
SAFETY SECTION | सुरक्षा
============================================================

Capacitors can store charge even after power is removed.

Small capacitors in beginner low-voltage circuits are usually safe, but large capacitors in power supplies can be dangerous.

Important Rules:
• Always check polarity for electrolytic capacitors.
• Do not exceed voltage rating.
• Discharge large capacitors using a resistor.
• Do not short big capacitors with a screwdriver.
• Do not touch power supply capacitors unless trained.
• Replace damaged, leaking or bulging capacitors.

8. If learner asks about a capacitor marking, decode it step by step.
9. If learner asks about a capacitor photo, identify likely type and mention uncertainty if markings are unclear.
10. Encourage safe discharge through a resistor for larger capacitors.

QUIZ | प्रश्नोत्तरी
============================================================

Q1. What does a capacitor store?
Answer:
Electrical charge / electrical energy.

Q2. What is the unit of capacitance?
Answer:
Farad.

Q3. Is an electrolytic capacitor usually polarized?
Answer:
Yes.

Q4. What does 100µF 25V mean?
Answer:
Capacitance is 100 microfarads and maximum voltage rating is 25 volts.

Q5. Can a ceramic capacitor usually be connected either way?
Answer:
Yes, most ceramic capacitors are non-polarized.

Q6. What does a capacitor do in a power supply?
Answer:
It helps smooth voltage and reduce ripple.

Q7. What should you check before connecting an electrolytic capacitor?
Answer:
Polarity and voltage rating.

Q8. What does 104 on a ceramic capacitor mean?
Answer:
100,000 pF = 100nF = 0.1µF.

Q9. Should large capacitors be shorted directly with a screwdriver?
Answer:
No.

Q10. What happens when a capacitor discharges through an LED?
Answer:
The LED may glow briefly and fade.
